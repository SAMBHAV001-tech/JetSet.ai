# Travel Guide: Tokyo, Japan (TYO)

## General Overview
Tokyo is Japan’s bustling capital, mixing ultra-modern skyscrapers with historic temples and shrines. It is known for its cleanliness, efficient transit, and outstanding culinary scene.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa):**
   * *Description:* Tokyo's oldest and most famous Buddhist temple, dedicated to Kannon Bosatsu.
   * *Hours:* Main hall open 06:00 AM – 05:00 PM daily (grounds open 24/7).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1.5–2 hours.
   * *Admission:* Free.

2. **Tokyo Skytree:**
   * *Description:* The tallest structure in Japan, offering panoramic views of the city (and Mt. Fuji on clear days).
   * *Hours:* 10:00 AM – 09:00 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 2 hours.
   * *Admission:* Approximately ¥1,800 – ¥3,100 depending on observatory packages.

3. **Meiji Jingu Shrine (Harajuku):**
   * *Description:* Shinto shrine dedicated to the deified spirits of Emperor Meiji and his wife.
   * *Hours:* Open Sunrise to Sunset daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1.5 hours.
   * *Admission:* Free.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Tokyo Subway Ticket:** Purchase the 24, 48, or 72-hour subway pass (available for tourists) to travel unlimited on Tokyo Metro and Toei lines.
* **Convenience Stores (Konbini):** Places like 7-Eleven, Lawson, and FamilyMart offer high-quality, cheap meals (bento, onigiri, katsu sandwiches) for budget travelers.
* **100 Yen Shops (Daiso):** Excellent for cheap souvenirs, adapters, and travel accessories.

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **Visa Exemption:** Many nationalities (US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia) enjoy visa-exempt stays up to 90 days.
* **Visit Japan Web:** Fill out customs and immigration details online before boarding to generate QR codes for fast-track entry.

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Earthquake Preparedness:** Earthquakes are common. Familiarize yourself with evacuation routes in your hotel.
* **Local Customs:** Do not walk and eat at the same time. Return your trash to your hotel, as public trash cans are extremely rare.
* **Emergency Numbers:** Dial 110 for police or 119 for ambulance/fire services.
