# Travel Guide: London, United Kingdom (LHR)

## General Overview
London, the capital of England and the United Kingdom, is a 21st-century city with history stretching back to Roman times. At its center stand the imposing Houses of Parliament, the iconic ‘Big Ben’ clock tower, and Westminster Abbey.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **British Museum:**
   * *Description:* A public museum dedicated to human history, art, and culture, housing the Rosetta Stone and Parthenon Sculptures.
   * *Hours:* 10:00 AM – 05:00 PM daily (Open until 08:30 PM on Fridays).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 3–4 hours.
   * *Admission:* Free (Pre-booking online ticket recommended).
   
2. **Tower of London:**
   * *Description:* Historic castle on the north bank of the River Thames, home to the Crown Jewels.
   * *Hours:* 09:00 AM – 05:30 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 2.5–3 hours.
   * *Admission:* Approximately £34.

3. **London Eye:**
   * *Description:* Giant cantilevered observation wheel on the South Bank of the River Thames.
   * *Hours:* 11:00 AM – 06:00 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 45 minutes.
   * *Admission:* Approximately £38-£42.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Oyster Card / Contactless Pay:** Tap in and out using contactless bank cards or mobile devices on the Tube and buses to benefit from fare capping. Never buy individual paper tickets.
* **Free Galleries & Museums:** Take advantage of free entry to permanent collections at the National Gallery, Tate Modern, Science Museum, and Natural History Museum.
* **Meal Deals:** Purchase sandwich meal deals (sandwich, snack, and drink for £3.50-£5.00) from local supermarkets (Tesco, Sainsbury's, Marks & Spencer) for budget lunches.

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **UK Standard Visitor Visa:** Required for passport holders of countries like India, China, and South Africa. Standard processing takes 3-6 weeks.
* **ETA (Electronic Travel Authorisation):** Visa-exempt nationalities will gradually require an ETA registration prior to flight check-in.

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Phone Snatching:** Stay alert on busy streets and near Tube exits (especially Oxford Street, Soho, and Westminster) where thieves on bicycles or mopeds grab phones.
* **Look Right/Left:** Pedestrian crossings have markers indicating which direction traffic is coming from, since vehicles drive on the left side of the road in the UK.
