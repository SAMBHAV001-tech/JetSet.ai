# Travel Guide: Paris, France (PAR)

## General Overview
Paris, the capital of France, is a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **Eiffel Tower (Tour Eiffel):**
   * *Description:* The iconic wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars.
   * *Hours:* 09:30 AM – 11:45 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 2–3 hours.
   * *Admission:* Approximately €18–€28 depending on lift access level.
   
2. **Louvre Museum (Musée du Louvre):**
   * *Description:* The world's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa and Venus de Milo.
   * *Hours:* 09:00 AM – 06:00 PM (Closed on Tuesdays; Open until 09:45 PM on Fridays).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 3–4 hours minimum.
   * *Admission:* €17 (Online pre-booking highly recommended).

3. **Arc de Triomphe:**
   * *Description:* Monument standing at the western end of the Champs-Élysées.
   * *Hours:* 10:00 AM – 11:00 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1 hour.
   * *Admission:* €13.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Navigo Easy Card:** Buy a weekly pass or a 10-ticket pack ("carnet") for the Metro to save heavily on individual transit tickets.
* **Paris Museum Pass:** If planning to visit more than 4 major museums, buy the pass to skip lines and save on entry.
* **Lunch Menus (Formules):** Order the fixed-price lunch menu at local bistros; it is usually 30-40% cheaper than the à la carte dinner.

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **Schengen Visa:** Travelers from non-EU countries like India, China, or South Africa require a Schengen Visa. Ensure passport validity is at least 3 months beyond the planned departure date.
* **ETIAS (Starting late 2026):** Visa-exempt nationalities (e.g., US, UK, Canada) must pre-register via the European Travel Information and Authorisation System (ETIAS).

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Pickpocketing Hubs:** Exercise high caution around the Eiffel Tower, Sacré-Cœur steps, Louvre area, and Metro Line 1. Keep bags zipped and phones secure.
* **Common Scams:** Avoid the "string trick" around Sacré-Cœur and people asking you to sign "petitions" near the Louvre.
* **Emergency Numbers:** Dial 112 for general European emergencies or 15 for medical help.
