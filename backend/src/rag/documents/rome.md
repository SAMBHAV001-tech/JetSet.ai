# Travel Guide: Rome, Italy (FCO)

## General Overview
Rome, Italy’s capital, is a sprawling, cosmopolitan city with nearly 3,000 years of globally influential art, architecture, and culture on display. Ancient ruins such as the Forum and the Colosseum evoke the power of the former Roman Empire.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **Colosseum & Roman Forum:**
   * *Description:* The iconic ancient amphitheater where gladiators fought, along with the adjacent ruins of Rome's ancient political center.
   * *Hours:* 08:30 AM – 07:15 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 3 hours.
   * *Admission:* Approximately €16–€22 (Tickets sell out weeks in advance; online booking mandatory).
   
2. **Vatican Museums & Sistine Chapel:**
   * *Description:* The massive museum complex of the Vatican City showcasing classical sculptures and Renaissance masterpieces (including Michelangelo's ceiling).
   * *Hours:* 08:00 AM – 07:00 PM (Closed on Sundays except last Sunday of the month).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 3–4 hours.
   * *Admission:* €20-€25 (Book early to skip queues).

3. **Trevi Fountain:**
   * *Description:* Spectacular Baroque fountain. Toss a coin in to guarantee a return trip to Rome!
   * *Hours:* Open 24/7 (Best visited early morning or late night to avoid massive crowds).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 30 minutes.
   * *Admission:* Free.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Free Water (Nasoni):** Do not buy plastic water bottles. Carry a reusable bottle and fill it for free from the small continuous-stream drinking fountains ("nasoni") located all over the city.
* **Roma Pass:** If staying for 48 or 72 hours, buy the Roma Pass for free entry to your first 1-2 museums, free public transit, and discounts on subsequent sites.
* **Eat Away from Piazzas:** Dining directly facing major squares (like Piazza Navona or Piazza Spagna) is extremely expensive. Walk 2-3 streets down for authentic, affordable Roman pastas (Carbonara, Cacio e Pepe).

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **Schengen Visa:** Standard Schengen Visa rules apply. Passport must be valid for at least 3 months after departure from Italy.

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Pickpockets on Transit:** Be extremely careful on Metro Line A, Bus 64 (Vatican route), and at Termini Station. Keep your hands on your belongings.
* **Gladiator Scams:** Do not take photos with costumed actors ("Gladiators") outside the Colosseum; they will demand €20-€50 for a photo.
