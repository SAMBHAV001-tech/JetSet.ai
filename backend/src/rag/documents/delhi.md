# Travel Guide: Delhi, India (DEL)

## General Overview
Delhi, India’s capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the country’s north. In Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating to the 1600s, stands the imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **Qutub Minar:**
   * *Description:* A 73m-tall towering minaret built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak.
   * *Hours:* 07:00 AM – 05:00 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1.5–2 hours.
   * *Admission:* Approximately ₹600 for foreigners, ₹40 for Indians.
   
2. **Humayun's Tomb:**
   * *Description:* The tomb of the Mughal Emperor Humayun, built in 1569-70. Landmark red sandstone mausoleum.
   * *Hours:* 06:00 AM – 06:00 PM daily.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 2 hours.
   * *Admission:* ₹600 for foreigners, ₹40 for Indians.

3. **India Gate:**
   * *Description:* War memorial dedicated to soldiers of the British Indian Army who died in the First World War.
   * *Hours:* Open 24/7 (Best visited in the evening when illuminated).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1 hour.
   * *Admission:* Free.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Delhi Metro Smart Card:** Buy a Tourist Metro Card (available for 1 day or 3 days) for unlimited rides across the clean, air-conditioned metro network.
* **Street Food Dining:** Eat at reputable street-food hubs in Old Delhi (Chandni Chowk) or Connaught Place outer circle for delicious, cheap meals.
* **National Museum Combo:** Look for student discounts or institutional passes if visiting government art galleries and museums.

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **Indian e-Visa:** Most international tourists require an e-Tourist Visa pre-approved online at least 4 days before travel. Ensure passport has at least 6 months validity.
* **Customs Declaration:** Keep cash declarations ready if carrying over USD 5,000 in cash or equivalent.

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Rickshaw Scam:** Agree on prices before entering auto-rickshaws, or better, use apps like Uber, Ola, or Namma Yatri for metered prices.
* **Delhi Belly:** Drink only bottled mineral water. Avoid ice in drinks and raw salads from street stalls.
* **Air Pollution:** In late autumn (November-December), air quality can deteriorate; carry an N95 mask if sensitive.
