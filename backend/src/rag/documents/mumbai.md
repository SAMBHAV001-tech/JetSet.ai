# Travel Guide: Mumbai, India (BOM)

## General Overview
Mumbai (formerly Bombay) is a densely populated city on India’s west coast. A financial center, it's India's largest city. On the Mumbai Harbour waterfront stands the iconic Gateway of India stone arch, built by the British Raj in 1924.

## Top Landmarks & Attractions
1. **Gateway of India:**
   * *Description:* Iconic basalt arch overlooking the Arabian Sea, built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary.
   * *Hours:* Open 24/7.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 45 minutes.
   * *Admission:* Free.
   
2. **Elephanta Caves:**
   * *Description:* UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring cave temples carved out of solid rock, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Located on Elephanta Island (accessed via ferry from Gateway of India).
   * *Hours:* 09:00 AM – 05:30 PM (Closed on Mondays).
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 3–4 hours (including ferry travel).
   * *Admission:* Ferry approx ₹260, Cave entry ₹600 for foreigners, ₹40 for Indians.

3. **Marine Drive:**
   * *Description:* A 3.6-kilometre-long C-shaped boulevard along the coast, popular for evening walks and sunset views.
   * *Hours:* Open 24/7.
   * *Estimated Visit Duration:* 1-2 hours.
   * *Admission:* Free.

## Budget & Cost-Saving Tips
* **Local Train Off-Peak Travel:** Use local trains for long north-south transits, but avoid peak rush hours (08:30 AM–11:00 AM and 05:30 PM–08:30 PM) to ensure safety.
* **Kaali-Peeli Taxis:** Black-and-yellow taxis operate strictly by digital meters. Always insist on using the meter or use ride-hailing apps (Uber, Ola).
* **Street Dining:** Enjoy local snacks like Vada Pav, Misal Pav, or Bhel Puri from clean, busy stalls at Juhu Beach or Girgaon Chowpatty.

## Visa & Entry Documentation Requirements
* **Indian e-Visa:** Same entry requirements apply as for Delhi. Non-citizens must secure e-Tourist Visas online before landing.

## Safety & Local Advisories
* **Monsoon Season:** Heavy rains between June and September can cause localized flooding and disrupt train schedules. Plan weather buffers.
* **Crowds:** Tourist spots and railway stations are extremely crowded; secure backpacks in front of you.
